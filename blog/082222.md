This had been a source of frustration for a while, now. Whenever I logged into a machine through SSH, it was extremely complicated to get its content to my clipboard. With time, I realised this would have to happen at a terminal level (I use `kitty`). Well, it took me *way too long* to just go ahead and google it. It seems like the best solution is to enable `copy_on_select yes` on my `kitty.conf`, and now it behaves exactly as one assumes: if I select with mouse on the remote machine, it goes into the main frame's clipboard. # Another scheme exercise 

Produce `deep-reverse` as such:

```scheme
(define x (list (list 1 2) (list 3 4)))
x
((1 2) (3 4))
(reverse x)
((3 4) (1 2))
(deep-reverse x)
((4 3) (2 1))
```

Well, this was, again, rather easy, except for the misc. syntax errors that seem to plague my unexperienced Scheme skills.

```scheme
(define (deep-reverse args)
  (define (iter-proc res args)
    (if (null? args)
        res
        (iter-proc (if (list? (car args))
                       (append (list (deep-reverse (car args)))
                              res)
                       (append (list(car args))
                              res))
                   (cdr args))))
  (iter-proc (list) args))
```

(define (pretty-deep-reverse args)
